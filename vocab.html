    Assets:

    An asset is anything that holds value and can be converted to money.

    Bank Statement:

    A bank statement is a record of all activity on a bank account.

    Budget:

    A budget is a plan for using your money to make sure all your financial responsibilities are met.

    Comparison Shopping:

    Comparison shopping is a strategy where you compare prices of similar products to find the cheapest one.

    Consumer:

    A consumer is someone who buys the products or services of another person/business.

    Debt:

    Debt is money you need to pay back to someone / money owed.

    Diversification:

    Diversification is a strategy to reduce risk by investing in various different assets.

    Dividends:

    Money earned from owning a company’s stock.

    Down Payment:

    A down payment is an amount given as security before the full payment is made.

    Interest:

    Interest is a certain percentage of the principal amount that lenders change on top of the principal amount.

    Loss:

    Loss is the net amount lost during a business transaction.

    Monopoly:

    A monopoly is situation where there is only one producer of a product or service.

    Net Income:

    Net Income is the total income minus all the expenses that need to be paid with it. Let’s say you earned $10 but you owed $2 to a friend so after paying back, your net income would be $8.

    Principal:

    Principal is the amount of money due on a loan without the interest being added.

    Profit:

    Profit is the net amount earned/gained during a business transaction.
